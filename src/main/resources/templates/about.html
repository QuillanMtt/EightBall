About the Game of Pool

Pool is a popular cue sport enjoyed worldwide. It combines strategy, skill, and precision, with players aiming to pocket balls in a specific order on a table with six pockets. Whether for leisure or professional tournaments, pool brings excitement to every game.

History

Pool evolved from outdoor lawn games in Europe during the 15th century. It became a popular indoor pastime in the 19th century and is now a professional sport worldwide.

Popular Games

  • 8-Ball – Pocket all your balls (solids or stripes) then the 8-ball
  • 9-Ball – Pocket balls in numerical order to win
  • Straight Pool – Score points by pocketing balls without following sequence

Equipment

  • Pool cue – used to strike the cue ball
  • Table – usually with six pockets and felt surface
  • Chalk – applied to the cue tip for better friction
  • Balls – numbered and colored according to the game

How to Play Pool

1. Place the balls in the correct rack for your game.
2. Break the rack with the cue ball.
3. Take turns with your opponent to pocket balls.
4. Use strategy and angles to plan shots ahead.
5. The game ends when all required balls are pocketed according to the rules.

Why People Love Pool

Pool is not only competitive but also social. It improves hand-eye coordination, mental calculation, and provides hours of fun.
